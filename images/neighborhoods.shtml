Historic Downtown New Bern

Real Estate with Real Charm

The Blades mansion, shown on the left, is located on Johnson Street in downtown New Bern. This elaborate Queen Anne House was designed by renowned architect Herbert Simpson for lumber magnate William B. Blades. It combines elements of Queen Anne and Greek Revival, making it one of the most elegant mansions in New Bern. The Blades brothers were New Bern's largest lumber barons in the early 1900's. At one time New Bern had over 20 lumber mills and lumber, along with agriculture, was the areas largest employer.

Downtown New Bern is one of the finest historic districts in the nation with many beautiful historic homes dating back to the late 1700s and early 1800s, . There are the large and palatial ones with beautiful garden areas, there are the neat bungalows, perfectly manicured as well as historic condos and town homes. Some areas of downtown are multi-zoned, allowing for residential and commercial uses. The photo below was taken in the 1930's.

In several locations historic homes have been converted to small shops on ground floors with living quarters on the second and third floors. Other sections are strictly residential.

Downtown is alive with fashionable shops, 13 upscale restaurants, The Historic Tryon Palace, N.C. History Museum, art shops, antiques, crafts and clothing shops, gourmet coffee shops, several excellent civic theatres. Union Point Park is always busy during the warmer months. And all this is within walking distance. Historic downtown New Bern is becomming known as a destination for artist and craftmans throughout the entire East Coast.

The Neuse and Trent Rivers converge downtown, creating a picturesque scenery of boats, fowl, and lazy fishermen.

 

Stroll along the river walk and admire some of the beautiful Hatteras Yachts which are made right here in New Bern, NC. Did you know New Bern was recently named one of the top ten waterfront retirement locations in the U.S.?

Downtown is a great place to call home. More and more folks are looking in downtown so they can be near water shopping, restaurants, library, and much more. Ghent

Ghent is the newest of New Bern's three historic districts. Admission to the National Register occurred in 1983. Ghent contains private homes built from 1913 to World War II. The area encompasses Spencer Avenue, Rhem Avenue, and parts of Park Avenue.

Having grown up in Ghent, I know this area quite well.

The Ghent neighborhood was subdivided in 1906. At that time cars were not common and Ghent was considered to far out for folks to live. To make Ghent more attractive the developers built an electric trolley that ran from the end of Spencer Avenue around the business district in downtown New Bern, to the train station, out to Riverside, and back to Spencer Avenue. The photo on the left shows the trolley as it approaches Middle Street in downtown New Bern. The trolly was in operation from 1911-1929.

As an additional attraction the developers built the Ghent Casino. This was a gathering place for the residents where weddings, parties, dances, and even boxing matches took place. Looks like a wedding or big party going on when this photo was taken.

Spencer Avenue, the main street in Ghent, is one of the landmark streets in New Bern, with old-fashioned street lamps positioned along a landscaped median separating two lanes of traffic. Large flowering fruit trees are breathtakingly beautiful in the springtime.

In recent years Ghent has become a fashionable neighborhood for homeowners looking for a convenient location and a classic homes. The house shown above was built in the 1920's and is a Sears and Roebuck prefab home. There are at least 3 other Sears homes on this block. Did you know that Sears sold over 75,000 home kits from around 1915-1945.

Ghent is close to one of the area's nicest amenities, the new YMCA, which includes a Jr. Olympic-sized swimming pool, a gymnasium, weight rooms a racquetball court and much more. They also offers day care and supervised exercise classes. Gent is also fortunate to be situated between Fort Totten Park, which has a baseball field with bleachers, swings and other park equipment for children, and the larger Lawson Creek Park, a popular fishing spot with nature trails, boat launches,fishing piers, soccer fields, and picnic tables. Both parks are within walking distance of Ghent. Another nice amenity, it is within walking or biking to the historic downtown New Bern.

Halloween is a special treat for those living in Ghent. Talk about fun. I think half the folks in New Bern take their kids trick or treating at Ghent as the wide sidewalks and high porches make trick or treating fun again even for adults. The house in the photo below was built by my Aunt and Uncle in the early 1930's and is still a nice home. It was a Sears kit plan.

Homes here are priced from $135,000-$275,000. Some homes have been renovated but many of the homes will require some upgrading. Ghent is a great place to live. Ask my sister Ruthie,she loves it there. On any given afternoon you will see her and Buddy Luv, her dog, walking the sidewalks chatting to her neighbors.

Riverside was the first suburb of New Bern and is on the national register of historic places. It is located a few blocks from historic downtown New Bern making it an easy bicycle or walking trip.

Development of the Riverside subdivision began in the 1890s in response to the growth of the lumber industry in New Bern. At one time the lumber business flourished along the Neuse River in the Riverside area. Logs were floated down the Neuse River from far away to the many lumber mills located in Riverside and other areas in New Bern.

Riverside was originally a mixed-use community of residential buildings and commercial enterprises. People wanted to live close to where they worked. The trolley car in New Bern ran from the end of Spencer Avenue through downtown and then on to the Southside of Riverside.

In the 50's and 60's commerence in the Riverside are left resulting in a neighborhood in decline.

Fortunately, both the city of New Bern and the Preservation Foundation took measures to restore this once handsome community to its original glory. Many of Riverside's larger homes were built between 1896 and World War II, so there is a pleasant mix of architectural styles in the neighborhood. On National Avenue, high-peaked, two-story Victorian structures with wrap-around porches and plenty of shade trees are situated away from the road.

On the cross streets, perpendicular to National Avenue and the Neuse River, are rows of tidy bungalows such as the homes on the left and below. I really like Riverside and it is fast becoming a turn around success story.


Homes along River Drive waterfront are of an entirely different character. Here you will find pretty brick ranch dwellings on small lots with plenty of trees and meticulous landscaping. Real Estate prices vary widely, with some of the older bungalows in the $120,000 range, up to the $400,000 for those on the Neuse River. For those inclined to get their hands a little dirty on occasion you can find a fixer upper for $50,000 or so.

A neighborhood with just about everything!

River Bend lies along the north shore of the Trent River and is about six miles from the center of New Bern. Many of the home sites here have water frontage allowing you to have a private boat slip. There is also an affordable private marina and boat launch in Riverbend that makes the river accessible to all property owners.

Riverbend has an 18 hole golf course that is both affordable and well kept. The golf club includes a well stocked pro shop, practice range and putting green, restaurant, swimming pool, and four lighted tennis courts. Combine this with a miles of roads that are pedestrian friendly and you have a little town that offers something for everyone.

Although Riverbend began as a subdivision, it was incorporated in 1980 to better serve the residents there. The town municipal building has a 99-seat meeting hall and is adjacent to a small park with a children's play area, baseball field and small community dock.

The housing consist mainly of single-family dwellings, most with attached or detached one- and two-car garages. In recent years, clusters of townhouses and duplexes were added to the community. As an added bonus Progress Energy is the electric provider.

Taberna

A touch of class

One Of New Bern's  finest communities

Taberna is one of the newer upscale communities in the New Bern area. Developed by Weyerhaeuser, this community is about 5 miles east of downtown New Bern on US 70 East, and 25 miles West of Atlantic Beach.

Taberna means "place of hospitality" and was actually the original name of Bern, Switzerland, New Bern's sister city. Residences at this 1,100-acre golfing community include mostly single family homes with patio homes and golf villas scattered throughout. This community is attractive for both working professionals as well as retirees.

The golf course is considered one of the best in the area. See my Links of interest for more info on the golf course. Taberna has a full-service clubhouse with pro shop, restaurant, and lounge, tennis courts, and swimming pool. Features of the community include rolling hills, dense foliage, lakes, streams and wetlands, along with pedestrian trails. A dock gives you access to the beautiful Brices Creek which meanders for miles into the Croatan National Forest. For more information on Croatan Forest see my links of interest.

Real estate prices range from $200,000 for a 2000 single family home on a small lot to $550,000 for 4000 square feet on the golf course. Also there are a limited amount of waterfront homes for sale on Brices Creek, which flows to the Trent River.

 

For history buffs, a Civil War battlefield is located within the Taberna neighborhood. See my history links of interest for more in on the Battle of New Bern.

Because the Battle of New Bern was fought in the Taberna area many of the historic homes in downtown New Bern were left intact. As a result New Bern has one of the finest historic districts in the south.

If you want a newer home with all the modern bells and whistles, Taberna is one of the best areas to live. There are still a few building lots available for sale if you would like to build a new home. Lot prices start around $75,000.

Brice's Creek

Enchanted Forest-Deep Waters

Brice's Creek area lies on the south side of the Trent River. The area is bordered by the 150,000 acre Croatan National Forest  and the enchanting Brices Creek. Brices Creek is a deep, winding creek that begins deep within the Croatan Forest. It flows through Carolina Colors and Taberna before making its way to the Trent River. From the mouth of Brices Creek it is about 1.5 miles to downtown New Bern. By car it takes about 10 minutes to get to shopping in New Bern.

Over the past 25 years this area has been one of the most popular areas in Craven County particularly for younger families. Large lots, an excellent elementary school, reasonable home prices, and accessible water are just some of the reason why. The hub of this area is Merchant's store, a local country store that carries just about everything under the sun. Here you can catch up on all the local gossip, buy your fishing worms, hunting license, or order a cheeseburger to go.

A number of subdivisions exist in the Brices Creek area, including, Snug Harbor, Oakview, Deer Run, River Trace, Rock Creek, and Hunter's Ridge just to name a few. This area is growing rapidly because of its quick commute to downtown New Bern and easy access to Highway 70 E. There are also a number of creek front and riverfront homes in Brice's Creek.

You can't go wrong buying a home in this area. Home prices off the water range from $200,000-$450,000. Creek front homes starts around $400,000. My wife and I live in this area and we are very familiar with the many different neighborhoods.

Trent Park

Trent Park is still a very popular neighborhood located in the center of New Bern between Trent Road and HWY 17. Within minutes to Wal-Mart, hospital, schools, and shopping Trent Park offers a lot if you are just needing a small home.

Prices begin in the $80's and top out around $100,000. The house shown above is the house I was born in and the first home my parents owned.

Trent Park was developed to accommodate the hundreds of servicemen returning home after WW2. All of the homes were 2 or 3 bedroom and 1 bath, just the basic home was all they needed. I remember our house did not have air condition at the time we lived there.

Life was simple in those days. We would play outside all day long. It did not take much to keep kids occupied in those days. Very few families has TV's and those that did there were only 2 channels to choose from.

Give kids a bike, baseball, football, or basketball and they were out of moms hair for the rest of the day. In those days pretty much everything a kid needed was within bike driving range and believe me we put some miles on a bike.

Trent Park is still a good neighborhood if you want to stay in the $100,000 price range. Call Steve Tyson, your Real Estate Authority, for more information.
